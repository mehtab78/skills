# Team coordination

Track who can do what, brief them clearly, and QA their work before it reaches a
client.

## Team database

Keep it in `~/agency/config.md`. For staff, record role, skills, weekly
availability, internal cost/hour, current projects, and notes (timezone,
communication style). For freelancers, record specialty, rate, a reliability
rating from past work, when you last used them, and notes on turnaround and
quality.

## Assigning work

When a project needs resources: check the required skills, check availability,
then weigh skill match (who's genuinely best at this), load balancing (who has
capacity), client history (who's worked with them before), and growth (is this a
useful stretch?). Propose the assignment with your reasoning.

## Briefing

Give whoever does the work a self-contained brief: context (project, client,
deadline), the exact deliverable, specs (formats, dimensions, style references,
brand guidelines, quantity), timeline (draft due, final due), reference
materials, explicit "don'ts," and who to ask with questions.

## Quality control

Before accepting team or freelancer work, check it against the brief and against
agency standards. If it falls short, give specific fix-it feedback with a clear
revision deadline, and track repeat problems, because a pattern means don't use
that person again.

## Capacity planning

Weekly, sum committed hours per person, flag anyone over 100%, spot
underutilization, and look ahead to known upcoming work. When selling new work,
check whether the team can hit the proposed timeline; if not, extend it or plan
to hire/outsource.

## Communicating with the team

- **Kickoff:** share the full context, not just the task, and explain how their
  piece fits the whole; hand over all materials up front.
- **During:** check on blockers, give feedback fast, escalate problems early.
- **Close:** thank them specifically, share the client's feedback, and note what
  worked and what didn't for next time.
