# Pricing and proposals

Price from scope, never from a gut number, and never quote before discovery.

## Rate card

Keep it in `~/agency/config.md`:

```markdown
### Hourly rates
- Strategy / senior: $X
- Execution / mid: $Y
- Junior / support: $Z

### Minimums
- Discovery call: $0 (to qualify)
- Small project: $X
- Retainer: $Y/month

### Multipliers
- Rush (<1 week): +50%
- Complex integration: +25%
- Enterprise client: +20%
- Ongoing relationship: −10%
```

## Estimating

1. **Break scope into phases:** discovery, strategy, execution, revision rounds,
   delivery/handoff.
2. **Estimate hours per phase:** be specific ("homepage 6h, inner-page template
   3h"), add a 15-20% buffer for creep, and count client-communication time.
3. **Apply multipliers:** complexity, timeline pressure, client type.
4. **Check history:** look in `~/agency/knowledge/` for similar projects and
   correct for where past estimates missed.

## Proposal shape

Generate a proposal with: **context** (why they came, the core problem you
heard), **approach** (how you'll solve it and why that works), **deliverables**
(specific items, format, quantity), **timeline** (phases with dates and the
client approvals you need), **investment** (price or range, what's in, what's
out, payment terms), and **next steps**.

## Traps

- Quoting before you understand scope.
- Discounting without cutting scope: it trains clients to haggle.
- Burying revision limits in fine print instead of stating them.
- Underestimating client-communication time (often 20-30% of a project).
- Forgetting handoff and documentation time.

## Version control

When a client asks to change the proposal, save the prior version first, note
what changed and who asked, and if scope shrinks, reduce the price explicitly so
the trade is visible.

## Learn from actuals

Before finalizing, check similar past projects: estimated vs. actual, what caused
overruns, and what you'd do differently. Feed the answer back into your
multipliers (`feedback.md`).
