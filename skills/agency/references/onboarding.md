# Client onboarding

A good intake turns a vague inquiry into a scoped, deliverable project, and
catches the problems before they become a bad contract.

## Intake

When an inquiry arrives (email, audio, call notes), pull out the essentials:
business/project name, the core need, budget (stated or implied), timeline, the
decision maker and stakeholders, and how they found you.

Then shape it into a structured brief:

- **Problem:** what's broken or missing.
- **Success criteria:** how everyone will know it worked.
- **Scope boundaries:** explicitly in and out.
- **Constraints:** tech, brand, legal, time.
- **Deliverables:** each with acceptance criteria.

## Catch the red flags early

- "ASAP" with no real deadline → pin down the actual date.
- Scope bigger than the budget implies → validate the budget range.
- No clear decision maker → identify one before proposing.
- Requirements shifting during intake → version what was said.
- War stories about a past agency → probe what actually went wrong.

## Discovery questions by type

- **Marketing/content:** current channels and performance, brand assets,
  competitors they admire or dislike, internal approval process.
- **Development:** existing stack, expected user volumes, integrations,
  maintenance expectations.
- **Design/creative:** available brand assets, examples they like, the approval
  chain, usage rights needed.
- **Consulting:** who commissioned this and why now, prior attempts, available
  data/access, capacity to implement afterward.

## Open a client file

Create `~/agency/clients/[client-slug].md` with status (prospect/active/past),
start date, primary contact, brand/voice notes, a project list, and a running
history log.

## Templates for the awkward conversations

- **Budget mismatch:** *"For the scope described, our typical range is X-Y. We
  can trim scope to fit [their budget], or is there flexibility on budget?"*
- **Unclear approver:** *"So this runs smoothly, who gives final sign-off on
  [deliverables]? I want them looped in at the right checkpoints."*
- **Rush:** *"We can hit a faster timeline at a [X]% rush rate; the standard
  timeline for this scope is [Y weeks]. Which suits you?"*
