# Writing proposals

Most proposals lose in the first line. The client is skimming dozens; yours has
to prove instantly that you read *their* post and can do *this* job.

## A shape that works

1. **Hook:** reference something specific from the job post, so it's obvious you
   actually read it.
2. **Proof:** one or two directly relevant samples, not your whole résumé.
3. **Approach:** a couple of lines on how you'd tackle *this* project.
4. **Next step:** a concrete ask, e.g. "a 15-minute call to lock down scope?"

## What sinks a proposal

- "Dear Hiring Manager" and other generic openers
- A template pasted in with no customization
- Listing every skill instead of the relevant ones
- Asking things the post already answered
- Quoting a price before you understand the scope

## Pricing the bid

| Situation | Move |
|-----------|------|
| Crowded post (50+ proposals) | Enter slightly under rate, plan to upsell |
| Direct invitation | Charge a premium: they came to you |
| Complex or fuzzy scope | "Happy to firm up pricing after a quick call" |
| Budget unclear | Offer a range and ask the clarifying questions |

## Spending Connects wisely

- The first couple of hours on a Best Match job convert best, be early.
- Direct invitations don't cost Connects; reply fast.
- Skip posts with 50+ proposals unless you're a near-perfect fit.
- Don't boost a bid until your profile and history can back it up.
