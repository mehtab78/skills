# Compliance & boundaries

Upwork enforces its terms harder than most platforms, and the penalties escalate
to frozen funds and permanent bans. Stay well inside the lines.

## Not allowed

- Automated login or session handling
- Auto-sending proposals without a human reviewing them
- Scraping job posts or client data
- More than one account per person
- Payment outside Upwork (bank transfer, crypto, PayPal, etc.)
- Misrepresenting who actually does the work

## Fine to do

- Use AI to help draft: as long as a human reviews before sending
- Research and analyze public job posts
- Customize a template per proposal
- Track clients in your own CRM outside Upwork
- Use productivity tools that never touch your Upwork account

## What violations cost

| Violation | Consequence |
|-----------|-------------|
| Chargebacks above ~1% | Warning, then suspension |
| Off-platform payment | Permanent ban |
| Multiple accounts | All of them banned |
| Fake reviews | Account terminated |
| Circumventing the ToS | Funds frozen and banned |

## Your responsibilities

1. **Respect client AI rules.** If a client forbids AI assistance, honor it or
   decline the job.
2. **Guard confidential data.** Don't run a client's NDA-covered material through
   third-party AI tools.
3. **Be accurate.** Don't claim capabilities you don't have.
4. **Mind jurisdiction.** The client's location can set the legal requirements.
   GDPR applies for EU clients, for instance.

## Safe habits

- Keep negotiations and agreements in Upwork chat.
- Use funded milestones for payment protection.
- Put scope in writing before starting.
- Save important conversations.
- Never share your Upwork credentials with anyone, including tools.
