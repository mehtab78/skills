# Profile optimization

Your profile is what clients read before they decide whether your proposal is
worth opening. The title and first two bio lines do most of the work.

## Title

Pattern: `[Primary skill] | [Secondary skill] | [Differentiator]`. The
differentiator is what stops the scroll:

- "React Developer | Next.js Expert | 50+ E-commerce Builds"
- "Copywriter | Email Sequences | B2B SaaS"

## Bio

The first two lines are visible before the "more" cut, so front-load them:

- **Line 1:** what you do, who you help, and the result you deliver.
- **Line 2:** proof, years, project count, notable clients.
- **After that:** your skills, how you work, and a call to action.

Write it about the client's outcome, not your autobiography.

## Portfolio

- Six to eight pieces, tops: quality over volume.
- Lead with your strongest work.
- Show the *result*, not just the deliverable.
- Curate toward the jobs you actually want.

## Hourly rate

| Stage | Rate posture |
|-------|--------------|
| New (under ~$1k earned) | Lower end in a niche, to build reviews fast |
| Rising ($1k-10k) | Market rate, get selective |
| Established (>$10k) | Premium rate, lean on direct invitations |

## What drags a profile down

- A generic title like "Freelancer" or "Virtual Assistant"
- A bio about you instead of client outcomes
- No portfolio, or samples unrelated to your target work
- Availability status that flip-flops
