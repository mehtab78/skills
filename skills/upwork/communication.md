# Client communication

Speed and a written trail are your two best habits on Upwork. Reply fast, keep
everything in Upwork chat, and adapt these drafts to your own voice.

## Timing

- Aim for a first reply within a couple of hours: Upwork rewards responsiveness.
- Be consistent during your stated business hours.
- Weekend silence is fine if you've set the expectation.

## Drafts to adapt (not paste)

- **Scope creep:** *"Happy to add [feature]. It's outside the original scope, so
  I'd include it for $X, want me to update the milestone?"*
- **A delay:** *"Quick update: [reason] pushes delivery to [date] instead of
  [original]. I'll keep you posted, let me know if that affects anything on your
  side."*
- **Asking for a review:** *"Really enjoyed working on this. If you have a
  moment, a review would mean a lot, no pressure either way."*

## Negotiation basics

- Don't negotiate against yourself: state your number and let them counter.
- Justify with value: *"My rate is X because [what they get]."*
- Walk away from scope creep that comes with no pay adjustment.
- Keep every agreement in Upwork chat: it's your protection if things go sideways.

## Match the client's tone

| Client | Register |
|--------|----------|
| Corporate | Formal, structured, regular status updates |
| Startup | Direct, casual, fast back-and-forth |
| Creative | Personable and collaborative |
