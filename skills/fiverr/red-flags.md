# Red flags: protecting your Fiverr account

Some buyers cost you more than they pay. Learn to spot them before you commit,
and keep every conversation on-platform where Fiverr's protections apply.

## Decline these outright

| Signal | Why it's dangerous |
|--------|--------------------|
| "Let's move to WhatsApp/email first" | Off-platform = no protection, common scam setup, and a ToS violation |
| "Send the work, I'll pay after I review it" | You won't get paid |
| Account made today + a big, complex order | High chargeback risk |
| "Give me extra free work and I'll leave 5 stars" | Review manipulation |
| Asks for your login details | Account theft |
| Vague requirements + a tight deadline | Guaranteed scope creep and a dispute |
| Wants a free "test" before ordering | Free-work extraction |

## Proceed carefully

| Signal | How to protect yourself |
|--------|--------------------------|
| No photo or bio | Pin down detailed requirements up front |
| First-time buyer | Set clear milestones and update often |
| Budget well below your rate | Decline politely, or explain the value gap |
| Wants unlimited revisions | Put a revision cap in writing before starting |
| History of cancellations (if visible) | Weigh whether it's worth the risk |

## Reply templates

Rewrite these in your own voice, they're starting points, not scripts.

- **Off-platform request:** *"Thanks for reaching out! I keep everything on
  Fiverr so we're both covered by their protections. Happy to answer anything
  right here, what would you like to know about the project?"*
- **Free-test request:** *"Totally get wanting to see my work. My portfolio and
  reviews show the style and quality; if you'd like a low-risk start, I can set
  up a small paid trial with a limited scope."*
- **Scope creep:** *"Good idea, that's a bit beyond this order, but I can add it
  as a custom offer for $X, or roll it into a follow-up. Which do you prefer?"*
- **Review threat:** *"I want you happy with this. Let's fix what's within the
  order scope, tell me the specific change and I'll sort it. Fiverr support can
  also help if you'd like them involved."*

## Preventing chargebacks

1. Keep all requirements and approvals in Fiverr messages, never external chat.
2. Deliver through Fiverr's Deliver button, not side channels.
3. Watermark previews until the order is complete.
4. Screenshot anything important that was agreed in chat.
5. Split large jobs into milestones or several smaller orders.

## Don't get yourself banned

Rules that are on *you* to follow:

| Action | Consequence |
|--------|-------------|
| Sharing contact info before an order | Warning, then ban |
| Taking payment outside Fiverr | Permanent ban |
| Fake reviews (friends or purchased) | Permanent ban |
| Running multiple accounts | Both banned |
| Delivering via sketchy external links | Order cancelled, possible ban |
| Pushing a competitor platform | Warning |
