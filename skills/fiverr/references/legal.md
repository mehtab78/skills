# Legal & AI-disclosure notes for Fiverr work

Not legal advice, general orientation. When real money or IP is at stake, check
Fiverr's current terms and, where it matters, a lawyer.

## Fiverr's stance on AI

Fiverr permits AI tools across every service category, provided the work is done
ethically and transparently. What that asks of you:

1. **Own the rights** to everything you deliver.
2. **Follow your AI tool's terms** of service.
3. **Add real human value:** no mass-produced, identical outputs.
4. **Be honest when asked:** never deny AI use if a buyer asks directly.

Off-limits regardless of category: deepfakes or impersonation without consent,
fraudulent documents, content that misleads about identity, political
manipulation, and automated decisions in sensitive domains (law enforcement,
finance, housing, employment).

## When you have to disclose

| Situation | What you owe |
|-----------|--------------|
| Buyer never asks about AI | No disclosure required |
| Buyer asks if you used AI | Answer truthfully: always |
| Your gig description | Optional, but a transparency note builds trust |

A simple, honest gig-FAQ line works: that you use professional tools including AI
assistance, that you review and customize everything yourself, and that you're
happy to explain your workflow.

## Who owns AI output, on Fiverr specifically

**Fiverr's terms hand the buyer full rights on payment**, which means you must
actually own those rights before you can pass them on. Pure AI output may not be
copyrightable in the first place (this varies by country), so if you rely on it
heavily, meaningfully edit it before delivery, the edited version is a far more
defensible and transferable deliverable. Also confirm your AI tool allows
commercial resale before you lean on it: most paid assistants do, but some
image/code models carry license conditions worth checking.

For the general cross-jurisdiction copyright and disclosure framework (what
counts as copyrightable, EU/US/UK differences, when disclosure is legally
required rather than just good practice), see the `freelance` skill's
`references/legal.md` — that's the canonical version this file builds on top of.

## Fiverr vs Upwork on AI

| Factor | Fiverr | Upwork |
|--------|--------|--------|
| AI policy | Explicitly permitted | More restrictive |
| Disclosure | When asked | Better to be proactive |
| Automation tools | OK for your own workflow | Prohibited for platform access |
| Enforcement | Moderate | Aggressive |

Bottom line: Fiverr is the more AI-friendly of the two. On Upwork, lean
conservative, see the `upwork` and `freelance` skills.
