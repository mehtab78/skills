# Scaling Fiverr operations (20+ orders a month)

Past ~20 orders a month, the bottleneck stops being demand and becomes
reliability. You need one place to see every order, replies that stay personal
at volume, and workflows that don't look automated to Fiverr.

## One view of every order

Track everything in a single board so nothing slips:

| Order | Gig | Buyer | Due | Status | Owner | Notes |
|-------|-----|-------|-----|--------|-------|-------|
| FO123 | Logo | @buyer1 | 4h | Revision | You | wants darker blue |
| FO456 | Video | @buyer2 | 18h | Waiting | VA-1 | needs source files |

Useful status buckets: **new** (reviewing requirements), **gathering** (waiting
on the buyer), **in progress**, **review** (your QA), **delivered** (awaiting
acceptance), **revision**, **complete**, **dispute**.

## Replies that scale without feeling canned

Keep templates, but never send the exact same text twice, write 3-5 variations
of each and rotate. For an order-received note, vary the opening, the promised
update time, and the clarifying question. The goal is genuinely personal
messages produced quickly, not one message copy-pasted forever.

**Timing matters too.** Instant replies every single time read as a bot. Let
responses land anywhere from a few minutes to half an hour during working hours,
allow the occasional longer gap, and set an away status off-hours.

## Bringing on help

When you hand an order to a VA or subcontractor, give them a clean brief so they
don't have to reconstruct it from the chat:

1. Requirements, tidied up from the buyer's messages
2. Reference files, downloaded and organized
3. The deadline, with a buffer before the real due time for your review
4. Style notes pulled from the conversation
5. Any red flags to watch for

**QA before every delivery:** matches all stated requirements · correct file
formats · professionally named files · checked for obvious errors · delivery
message drafted.

## The money side

- Fiverr takes a platform fee on earnings (including tips), budget for it.
- Cleared funds have a clearance period (shorter once you're Top Rated).
- Track a simple monthly P&L: gross revenue − Fiverr fees − VA costs − tool
  subscriptions = actual profit. Watch the margin, not just the top line.
- Compute your effective hourly rate per gig type (price ÷ hours, after fees). If
  a gig type earns below your target, raise its price or streamline the process.

## Staying on the right side of automation rules

Fiverr watches for patterns that signal automation or account abuse: identical
message text sent repeatedly, replies that are consistently near-instant, logins
from several IPs at once, and generally unusual activity.

Stay safe by using varied templates, natural delays, and one device/IP per
account, and by keeping a human approving every send. Never automate deliveries,
cancellations, or disputes, those must always be a person's decision.

## Rough scaling milestones

| Orders/month | Focus | Key moves |
|--------------|-------|-----------|
| 20-50 | Efficiency | Template library, basic order tracking |
| 50-100 | Delegation | First VA, a clear hand-off process |
| 100-200 | Systems | Full tracking, multiple VAs, real QA |
| 200+ | Growth | Upsells, and only legitimate account expansion |
