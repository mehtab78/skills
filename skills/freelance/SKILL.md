# Freelancing across platforms

Marketplace-agnostic decisions: where to sell, what to charge, how to pitch, and
how to stay compliant. For deep platform mechanics, hand off to the dedicated
skills: `fiverr` and `fiverr-gig-optimizer` for Fiverr, `upwork` for Upwork.

## Picking a platform

Match the platform to the kind of work, not hype:

| You want | Go to | Because |
|----------|-------|---------|
| Long-term contracts | Upwork | Escrow and hourly tracking |
| Quick productized gigs | Fiverr | Discovery is SEO-driven |
| Budget projects / contests | Freelancer.com | Lower fees, contest model |
| Premium, vetted work | Toptal | Curated top-tier talent |

More detail per platform (Connects, fees, levels, screening) is in
`platforms.md`.

## A proposal that wins (Upwork / Freelancer)

Keep it to roughly 150-250 words: **hook → plan → proof → ask.**

- Open by naming two specific things from the post, so they know you read it.
- Lay out your approach as a few concrete steps with acceptance criteria and a
  timeline.
- Cite a recent, relevant result with a number and a sample link.
- Close with a small first phase and a call to align.

Avoid: generic intros, vague timelines, no samples, and desperate underbidding.

## Setting and raising rates

- **New (0-3 months):** ~15-20% under market, optimizing for reviews.
- **Established (3-12 months):** market rate; bump ~10% every few projects.
- **Expert (12+ months):** premium pricing, and be selective.

A floor to sanity-check against: `(target annual income ÷ ~1500 billable hours)
× 1.25 = your minimum hourly`.

## Vetting the client

Walk away from any of these, on any platform:

- Unverified payment plus no hiring history
- "Work for exposure/equity" only
- Wants to go off-platform before there's a contract
- Asks for free test work up front
- Pay claims that are too good to be true

## AI disclosure, briefly

- **EU:** the AI Act generally requires disclosing AI-generated content to
  clients.
- **US:** no federal mandate, but the FTC prohibits deceptive AI claims.
- **Every platform:** their terms forbid full automation; a human must review
  and approve all proposals and deliverables.

The legal deep-dive (liability, copyright, fraud line) is in `legal.md`.
