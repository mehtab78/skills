# Platform reference

A side-by-side of the big four. Numbers drift, treat these as orientation and
confirm the current figures on each site.

## Upwork

- **Connects:** a few per proposal depending on job tier; a small free monthly
  allotment, more with paid plans. Boosting auctions for the top slots.
- **60-second triage:** score a job 0-5 on fit, scope, value, and client. Top
  scores → bid now (maybe boost); middling → bid today; low → skip and save
  Connects.
- **Metrics to watch:** reply rate above ~20%, win rate above ~10%, JSS above
  ~90%.
- **Profile essentials:** real headshot, keyword-rich niche title, a 300-500
  word summary (what you do, who you help, results with numbers), and 4-6
  portfolio pieces with context.

## Fiverr

- **Gig SEO:** keyword in title/description/tags/slug, click-through rate
  (thumbnail quality), conversion rate, and fast response time (under an hour is
  the target).
- **Levels (verify current bars):** Level 1 around 60 days + 10 orders + ~$400 +
  4.7 rating; Level 2 around 120 days + 50 orders + ~$2,000 + 4.7; Top Rated
  around 180 days + ~$20K + invitation.
- **Three-tier pricing:** Basic (minimal entry deliverable), Standard (your real
  full-service price), Premium (white-glove, roughly 1.5-2.5× Standard).
- **Gotcha:** the gig URL slug is locked after the first save, get it right.

For actually building the gigs, use `fiverr-gig-optimizer`; for selling and
scaling, use `fiverr`.

## Freelancer.com

- **Bids:** a small free monthly allowance, scaling up with paid tiers.
- **Projects vs. contests:** projects use traditional bidding with milestones;
  contests are spec work, only enter ones with a guaranteed, sealed prize.

## Toptal

- **Screening:** a multi-stage vetting gauntlet (language, skills test, live
  screening, a test project, then ongoing performance) with a low acceptance
  rate.
- **Economics:** no bidding, clients are matched to you, you set your own
  (typically premium) rate, and the client pool skews high-end.

## Automation rules (all platforms)

Every platform prohibits auto-submitting proposals at scale, mass messaging or
auto-responses, account sharing or impersonation, scraping, and bypassing rate
limits or captchas. What's allowed everywhere: AI-assisted drafting with a human
reviewing and sending manually.
