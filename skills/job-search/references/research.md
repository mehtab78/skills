# Researching companies

A little due diligence before applying saves you from ghost jobs, sinking ships,
and lowball offers. Do it before you spend effort tailoring an application.

## Is the company healthy?

- **Finances:** funding rounds and runway (Crunchbase), plus recent news for
  layoffs, hiring freezes, or acquisitions.
- **Headcount trend:** LinkedIn shows whether they're growing or shrinking.

## What's the culture like?

- **Glassdoor:** filter to the last ~6 months, old reviews describe a company
  that may no longer exist.
- **Tenure:** short average tenure on LinkedIn is a warning; so is churn in
  leadership.

## Red-flag checklist

| Check | Warning sign |
|-------|--------------|
| Posting age | 90+ days → ghost job or high turnover |
| Pay transparency | "DOE" or missing → likely lowball |
| Interview rounds | 5+ → an indecisive org |
| Review pattern | Recurring "great people, bad management" |
| Growth story | "Hyper-growth" but flat headcount |

## Evaluating an offer

Research pay against current data, Levels.fyi for tech, Glassdoor's salary tool,
and anonymous sources like Blind, and always sanity-check recency, since the
market has moved.

Then compare *total* compensation, not just base:

- **Equity:** vesting schedule, strike price, current valuation.
- **Benefits:** real healthcare cost, actual PTO usage.
- **Remote policy:** genuinely remote vs. "flexible" with strings.
- **Growth:** promotion velocity and learning budget.
