# Interview preparation

Preparation is mostly homework plus a handful of stories you can tell well. It
also runs both ways, you're screening them too.

## Before the interview

1. **Company:** funding, recent news, the product, the competitors.
2. **Interviewer:** LinkedIn, background, any shared connections.
3. **Role:** re-read the description and map your experience to each requirement.
4. **Your questions:** prepare 3-5 that show you did the research.

## Questions you'll likely get

**Behavioral** (answer in situation → task → action → result form):

| Question | What they're probing |
|----------|----------------------|
| "Tell me about yourself" | A tight 2-minute arc: past → present → why here |
| "Biggest challenge" | Problem-solving and resilience |
| "Conflict with a colleague" | Communication and maturity |
| "Why are you leaving?" | Positive framing, no badmouthing |

**If you're changing careers:** have an authentic reason for the switch, a
concrete ramp-up plan, and a clear statement of the fresh perspective you bring.

**If you're senior:** frame a move after several years as growth rather than
escape, give real examples of your management style, and show you can disagree
with leadership diplomatically but with a spine.

## Red flags *from* them

- An interviewer who's unprepared or late
- Can't explain the role clearly
- Evasive about compensation
- Bad-mouths former employees
- "We're like a family" (often means blurred boundaries)
- Pressure to decide on the spot

## After

1. Send a personalized thank-you within a day, not a template.
2. Log notes while fresh: what they asked, your read, any concerns.
3. Update `applications.md` with status and next steps.
4. Set a follow-up reminder if you don't hear back within their stated timeline.
