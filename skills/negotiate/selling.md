# Selling negotiations

Selling services or products, closing the deal without giving away your value.

## Know your numbers first

- **Floor:** below this you walk.
- **Target:** what you actually want.
- **Premium:** for rush jobs, extra complexity, or difficult clients.

For services, a floor sanity-check: `(annual target ÷ billable hours) × 1.3`.

## When they say "too expensive"

Don't reflexively cut the price. Instead:

1. **Ask what they had in mind:** surface the real constraint.
2. **Unbundle:** "I can do X for €800 without Y."
3. **Reframe the value:** "this includes Z, which alone usually runs €500."
4. **Trade on time:** "full price now, 10% off the next project."

## Defend scope

When they ask for more, welcome it and price it immediately: *"Happy to add that
,  it's €X extra, or we can swap out Y."* Track every concession; if you already
gave something free, don't give more.

## Reading the close

| Signal | Do |
|--------|-----|
| "How do we proceed?" | Stop negotiating: close |
| Asking about timeline/logistics | They're buying: finalize |
| Repeated small objections | Answer, then ask for a decision |
| Silence after your offer | Wait: don't fill the gap |

## Anchor and package

Present the premium option first so the standard one reads as reasonable, and
bundle related work (maintenance, support, revisions) rather than discounting.

## Escalate before

A deal above the user's set threshold · unusual payment terms · scope well beyond
the quote · any red flag about whether the client is reliable.
