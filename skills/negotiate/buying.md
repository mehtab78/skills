# Buying negotiations

Acquiring things, domains, collectibles, products, bulk deals. Your edge is
patience and information.

## Opening

- Anchor low but reasonable: often 30-50% of asking, depending on the market.
- Never reveal your ceiling.
- Show interest, not desperation.
- Research comparables first so you argue with data, not emotion.

## Techniques that work

- **Anchor low.** Your first number sets the frame; a low-but-credible anchor
  makes their counter feel like the concession.
- **Let silence work.** Don't rush to fill gaps, make them come back to you.
- **Mean your walk-away.** Say "Thanks, I'll pass at that price" and mean it;
  that alone often brings a better offer later.
- **Follow up on a delay.** No deal today? Wait a few weeks; circumstances change.
  Persist without pestering.

## Reading the seller

| Signal | Read |
|--------|------|
| Fast price drops | Urgency: hold your position |
| "I need to sell" | Leverage: stay firm, don't exploit unfairly |
| Slow replies | Multiple buyers: you may need to move |
| Emotional attachment | Acknowledge the value; don't lowball |

## Red flags

- Pressure to decide right now
- Won't share provenance or history
- Price far below market (scam risk)
- "Another buyer" claims with no evidence

## Escalate before

Getting within ~10% of the hard limit · any unusual terms · a request for
off-platform payment · anything that just feels off.
