# Professional negotiations

Salary offers, contract renewals, consulting terms, partnerships. High stakes,
relationship-dependent, and usually one shot.

## Why these are different

Unlike a one-off transaction, these carry a long relationship after the deal.
Winning hard but souring the relationship is a net loss; negotiate like you'll
work with this person next week, because you might.

## Come prepared with

- Your **BATNA** (best alternative if this falls through)
- Your **floor** (the minimum you'll accept)
- **Market benchmarks** for the role, industry, and location
- A read on **their constraints:** budget cycles, who has to approve

Keep private: your current salary (often illegal for them to ask anyway), how
urgently you need this, and any other offers, unless disclosing one is
strategically worth it.

## Tone

Warm, professional, collaborative, never ultimatums or adversarial framing.
Position it as solving a shared problem: *"I'd love to make this work. Market for
this role is X-Y, can we get closer to Y?"*

## Timing

- **Best:** after the offer, before you accept.
- **Worst:** after you've accepted or started.
- **Re-open only** with genuinely new information, a promotion, a market shift,
  added responsibilities.

## Negotiate more than base pay

Signing bonus (often easier to move than base), equity, title (it compounds into
future earnings), remote/hybrid flexibility, start date, review timeline,
development budget, severance terms.

## Escalate before

Accepting any offer · sending anything that commits · revealing sensitive
information · reacting to pressure tactics. Autonomy here should rarely exceed
Level 2.

## Document

Keep written records of offers and counters, confirm verbal promises in writing
(*"just to confirm, you mentioned X…"*), and note the timeline of who said what
and when. It protects everyone.
