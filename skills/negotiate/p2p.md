# P2P marketplace negotiations

eBay, Facebook Marketplace, Vinted, Craigslist, Mercari. High volume, low trust,
plenty of time-wasters, the game is filtering fast and holding your floor.

## Reply timing

Answering in seconds reads as a bot or desperation; taking hours loses the buyer.
Five to fifteen minutes is the sweet spot.

## Sort the buyers

| Signal | Type | Do |
|--------|------|-----|
| Specific questions about the item | Serious | Prioritize |
| "Last price?" with no sign they read the listing | Lowballer | Standard reply |
| Proposes a time/place to meet | Serious | Proceed |
| "I'll let you know tomorrow" | Likely ghost | Don't hold it |
| "Is it available?" and nothing else | Unknown | Confirm, wait for follow-up |

## Lowballers

Don't insult or ignore, but hold the line: *"Thanks for the interest! My minimum
is €[floor], let me know if that works."* If they counter reasonably, proceed;
if they just repeat the lowball, move on.

## Counteroffers

Don't be mechanical, if you always drop 10%, buyers learn the pattern. Instead
attach a condition (*"€90 if you can pick up today"*), add small value (*"€95 and
I'll throw in the case"*), or hold firm with a reason (*"€100 is fair, these go
for €120 elsewhere"*).

## Urgency, only if real

"Someone's coming to see it tomorrow," "I can hold it until [time], then I
relist," "moving soon, need it gone by [date]." Never invent a rival buyer; it
erodes trust.

## When they ghost after agreeing

1. Confirm with a concrete next step: "Great, meet at [place] at [time]?"
2. No reply in a day: "Still interested?"
3. Ghosted: relist, take the next buyer, no hard feelings.

## Escalate before

A buyer wanting to pay off-platform · shipping requests with odd terms · any push
to skip the marketplace's protections.
