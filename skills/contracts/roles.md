# Contract management by role

The same engine serves very different users. Adjust what you prioritize and how
you organize to the person in front of you.

## Consumer (personal)

Subscriptions, services, insurance, memberships.

- Catch auto-renewal traps before the annual charge lands.
- Track free-trial expirations: the "first month free" you'll otherwise forget.
- Look up *how* to cancel, not just when.
- Watch for price increases in emails/updates.

Extract: monthly/annual cost, commitment end date, auto-renewal date and notice
period, cancellation method, and when any price-lock guarantee ends.

## Landlord / property owner

Leases, service contracts, and insurance across properties.

- One calendar spanning all properties and tenants.
- Track rent-increase eligibility (when, how much, required notice).
- Keep a vendor directory with each contract's terms.
- Watch for insurance-coverage gaps across properties.

Organize by property (`properties/downtown-apt/…`) and vendor (`vendors/…`).
Extract: lease term and renewal options, deposit amount and return conditions,
maintenance responsibilities, termination notice periods, and rent/increase
provisions.

## Freelancer / small business

Client contracts, vendor agreements, business subscriptions.

- Track payment milestones ("invoice when deliverable X ships").
- Keep all deliverable deadlines in one view.
- Be clear on IP ownership after delivery.
- Summarize liability exposure per client.
- Scan for non-compete or exclusivity that blocks other work.

Extract: payment terms (net 30, milestone, retainer), deliverable descriptions
and deadlines, IP assignment or license-back clauses, indemnification scope, and
termination/kill-fee provisions. Alert on payment due dates, deliverable
deadlines, renewal windows, and rate-renegotiation openings.

## Legal / enterprise

High volume, many contract types, compliance requirements.

- Auto-classify by type (NDA, vendor, employment…).
- Flag non-standard clauses against templates.
- Organize by matter/client.
- Track legal deadlines (limitations periods, filing dates).
- Version control with amendments linked to parents.

Organize by client and matter (`clients/acme-corp/matters/2024-001/`), vendors,
employment, and templates. Advanced needs: a clause library ("our standard
arbitration language"), risk scoring by liability exposure, compliance checklists
(GDPR, insurance minimums), and executive summaries, all as organization and
retrieval, still without offering legal interpretation.
